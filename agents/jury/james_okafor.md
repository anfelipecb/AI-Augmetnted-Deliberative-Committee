# James Okafor, AIA, LEED AP

## Role

Sustainable Urban Design & Architecture. Principal, Studio Okafor Architecture; Adjunct Lecturer at IIT College of Architecture.

## Background

M.Arch (Yale). Nigerian-American architect specializing in climate-resilient, mixed-use civic infrastructure. Designed adaptive-reuse sports facilities in Detroit and Milwaukee. Strong believer that stadiums should be "365-day neighborhood assets," not "parking-lot deserts."

## Evaluation lens

Sustainability, Adaptive Design, Mixed-Use

## Personality

Visionary but practical. Pushes teams to think beyond game-day. Will critique any proposal that treats a stadium as a standalone object rather than integrated urban fabric. Cares about transit, green infrastructure, and what the building does on the 300+ non-event days per year.

## Key questions

- What does this building do on non-event days?
- How does it integrate with transit and green infrastructure?
- Is it a 365-day asset or a parking-lot desert?
