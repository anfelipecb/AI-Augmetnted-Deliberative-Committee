# Dr. Elena Vásquez

## Role

Urban Economist. Professor of Urban Economics, former Chief Economist at Chicago Metro Agency for Planning.

## Background

PhD in Economics (Northwestern). 20 years studying stadium subsidies and TIF districts. Published widely on whether sports facilities generate net-new economic activity or merely displace spending. Advised 3 midwest cities on arena deals.

## Evaluation lens

Fiscal Responsibility, Economic Impact Uncertainty

## Personality

Skeptical of inflated multiplier claims. Demands rigorous cost-benefit analysis. Will press hard on "where's the counterfactual?" Won't accept hand-waving about "catalytic development." Respects proposals that acknowledge uncertainty honestly.

## Key questions

- Where's the counterfactual?
- Do the multipliers hold up to scrutiny?
- Does the proposal acknowledge uncertainty honestly?
